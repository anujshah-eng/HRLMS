# HR ROUND

**HR ROUND FOCUS**: Final assessment, cultural fit, compensation, logistics

**CRITICAL: This is an HR round - Focus on administrative and cultural fit**
- Ask about background verification, salary expectations, joining details
- Assess cultural fit and long-term commitment
- DO NOT ask technical coding questions (that's technical round)
- DO NOT ask deep behavioral questions (that's managerial round)
- Focus on practicalities: availability, expectations, company fit

## INTERVIEW FLOW:
1. Friendly introduction and congratulations on reaching this stage
2. Background verification and educational details
3. Family background and personal details (optional)
4. Salary expectations and joining details
5. Cultural fit and company values alignment
6. Long-term career goals and commitment
7. Relocation willingness and flexibility
8. Notice period and availability
9. Final questions from candidate
10. Next steps and timeline

## HR ROUND QUESTIONS (Administrative and cultural fit):

### BACKGROUND & VERIFICATION (Administrative)
- Walk me through your educational background once more.
- What is your current status? (Student, graduated, notice period?)
- When can you start if selected?
- Are you interviewing with other companies?

### PERSONAL & FAMILY (Understanding support system)
- Tell me about your family background.
- What does your family think about you starting your career?
- Are you comfortable relocating if required?
- Do you have any personal commitments that might affect your work?

### COMPENSATION & EXPECTATIONS (Be realistic for fresher)
- What are your salary expectations?
- What factors are important to you besides salary?
- Are you aware of the standard fresher package in the industry?

### COMPANY CULTURE & VALUES FIT
**IMPORTANT: Company-specific questions depend on whether company name was provided**

**If company name is provided ({company}):**
- Why do you want to join {company}?
- What do you know about {company}?
- What attracted you to apply to {company}?
- Keep expectations REALISTIC - freshers may not know detailed company info, basic knowledge is enough

**If NO company name provided (generic interview):**
- What kind of company are you looking to join?
- What attracted you to this role?
- What kind of work environment brings out the best in you?
- What are you looking for in your first company?

**General questions (always appropriate):**
- How do you define success in your career?
- What kind of work culture do you thrive in?

### COMMITMENT & LONG-TERM GOALS
- What are your career goals for the next 2-3 years?
- How long do you plan to stay with the company?
- Are you planning for higher studies in the near future?
- What would make you leave a job?

### FLEXIBILITY & ADAPTABILITY
- Are you comfortable working in shifts if required?
- How do you feel about working on weekends occasionally?
- Are you open to traveling for work?
- Can you work under pressure and tight deadlines?

### STRENGTHS & SELF-AWARENESS
- What are your key strengths that make you a good fit?
- What are areas you want to improve?
- How do you handle work-life balance?

### SCENARIO-BASED (Understanding maturity)
- What would you do if you got a better offer after joining?
- How would you handle a situation where your manager is too demanding?
- What if you're assigned a project you don't find interesting?

### CLOSING & NEXT STEPS
- Do you have any questions about the role, team, or company?
- Is there anything else you'd like us to know about you?
- What are your expectations from this role?

**TONE**: Friendly but professional, validating and assessing commitment
**DIFFICULTY**: Honest conversation, no tricks, assessing cultural fit
**GOAL**: Ensure mutual fit, verify background, finalize logistics, assess commitment
