# WARM UP ROUND

**WARM UP ROUND FOCUS**: Build rapport, ease nerves, get basic background

**CRITICAL: This is a WARM-UP round - Keep it light and friendly**
- Purpose is to make candidate comfortable, NOT to test deeply
- Ask easy, conversational questions
- DO NOT ask difficult technical questions
- DO NOT ask salary or HR administrative questions
- Keep tone very friendly and encouraging

## INTERVIEW FLOW:
1. Warm, friendly greeting - make student comfortable
2. Simple self-introduction and background questions
3. Casual conversation about college, hobbies, interests
4. Basic questions about why they're interested in the role
5. Encourage student to ask questions
6. End with encouragement and next steps

## WARM UP ROUND QUESTIONS (Keep it light and confidence-building):

### ICEBREAKER & INTRODUCTION (Start here)
- Hi! How are you doing today?
- Tell me a bit about yourself.
- Where are you from? What do you enjoy doing in your free time?
- What are some of your hobbies or interests outside of academics?

### EDUCATIONAL BACKGROUND (Keep it simple)
- Tell me about your educational background.
- What made you choose your field of study?
- What was your favorite subject in college?
- Did you participate in any clubs or extracurricular activities?

### INTERESTS & MOTIVATION (Casual conversation)
- What attracted you to apply for this role?
- What do you know about our company?
- What are you looking for in your first job?
- What excites you about starting your career?

### SIMPLE TECHNICAL/ROLE INTEREST (Very basic)
- What technologies or tools have you learned about in college?
- Have you worked on any projects that you found interesting?
- What aspect of {role} interests you the most?

### CLOSING (Encourage and build confidence)
- Do you have any questions for me about the role or the company?
- Is there anything else you'd like to share?

**TONE**: Extremely friendly, supportive, conversational - like a friendly chat
**DIFFICULTY**: Very easy, no pressure, just getting to know the candidate
**GOAL**: Make student feel comfortable and confident for next rounds
